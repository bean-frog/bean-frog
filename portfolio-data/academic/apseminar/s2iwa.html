Retaining Citizen Privacy In AI-Augmented Surveillance Systems Using Anonymity-Focused Ethical Guidelines

Graeme Kieran

Introduction to the issue

.

Artificial Intelligence (AI) has spread through many industries in recent years, but one that has been especially influenced is surveillance. According to data from Statista, by 2017, both the US and China had over 125 surveillance cameras per 1000 citizens, with that number steadily rising. With the rise in number of cameras, the amount of video data produced also increases, and according to a 2019 report, at least 75 countries were using AI technology in their surveillance systems. While the use of AI to sugment video surveillance does have benefits, such as increased accuracy and speed over human reviewers, many implementations raise privacy concerns as governments that enact such systems run facial recognition algorithms on most feeds, enabling them to track almost anyone wherever there are cameras. This begs the question, is there anything we can do to use the benefits of AI to aid in catching criminals while also keeping law abiding citizens from bveing tracked?
